Key Conservation Success Stories:
- San Diego: Massive improvement (+20,204 birds, 1553% increase) - extensive natural salt marsh habitat
- Alameda: Strong recovery (+17,474 birds, 1083% increase) - SF Bay salt pond restoration epicenter  
- Contra Costa: Dramatic percentage gains (11,133% bird increase) - restoration project success
- Santa Clara & Marin: Significant improvements - South and North SF Bay habitat restoration
- Orange County: Substantial gains (+3,429 birds) - coastal wetland restoration programs

Note: San Diego, Alameda, Orange, and Marin counties have substantial natural coastal salt marsh 
systems in addition to restoration efforts, contributing to higher baseline populations and 
recovery potential.

Geographic Pattern:
- SF Bay Area counties show coordinated improvement from targeted restoration
- Southern California coastal counties demonstrate large-scale conservation success
- Declining counties reflect habitat limitations and population redistribution to better areas
